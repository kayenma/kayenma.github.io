Cambodia

Most Cambodians trace their ancestry to the Khmer people, who descended from the once-mighty Angkor Empire. However, the empire's decline began after being weakened by attacks from the Thai and Cham, resulting in a long period of decay. Cambodia was placed under French protection in 1863 and became part of French Indochina in 1887, but finally gained full independence from France in 1953 following Japanese occupation during World War II.

In March 1975, the communist Khmer Rouge seized Phnom Penh after a seven-year struggle, leading to the evacuation of all cities and towns. Under the Khmer Rouge regime, at least 1.5 million Cambodians perished from execution, forced labor, or starvation. A Vietnamese invasion in December 1978 ousted the Khmer Rouge from power and triggered a 10-year Vietnamese occupation, followed by two decades of civil war.

Today, Cambodia enjoys strong and burgeoning economic and political ties with its northern neighbor, China. Over 53% of foreign investment in the country in 2021 came from China, with Beijing providing over $15 billion in financial aid since the 1990s. The Chinese government also accounted for 443% of Cambodia's foreign debt in 2021. The ruling party, sees China's support as a way to counterbalance the influence of Thailand and Vietnam while also fending off international criticism of the human rights and democratic record.
